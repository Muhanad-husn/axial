# Academic outreach — everything in one place

Your control panel for what goes to outside academics. All of it lives in this
one folder so you can send it, track it, and follow up without digging.

## The source list you asked for

It is already in two places: the **appendix of `outreach-letter.md`** (so a
colleague who gets the letter sees exactly the 30 works the system reads), and the
standalone **`corpus-bibliography.md`**. Nothing to add.

The letter's appendix is honest about the corpus: it flags the two partial
holdings (Mann vol. 2 is ~63 pages of an ~800-page volume; Üngör is one chapter),
the master's paper, and the few unresolved bibliographic gaps. That honesty is a
feature when writing to specialists.

## How to run the research-question ask

1. Open `outreach-letter.md`. Personalize the four bracketed spots (name; how you
   came to their work; the optional question-area line; sign-off).
2. Send one per colleague. The appendix goes out unchanged.
3. As replies arrive, paste them to the operator session (me). I turn them into
   the versioned dev briefs the engine reads.

**The ask is small on purpose.** 2–3 questions per person, tier 1 sufficient,
across several colleagues. Do not chase a total. Whatever comes back, the build
adapts — the "26" in the spec is a soft target, not a quota.

## Open decisions before you send

The letter's own "Notes for the founder" section lists these. The two that matter:

- **Partial holdings (Mann vol. 2, Üngör).** Re-source both, or send with the
  appendix's honest description. Do not list either as a whole work. Mann vol. 2
  is the classes-and-nation-states volume, the one most likely to be central to
  the questions you get back.
- **The Kandiah master's paper** sits beside Princeton and Cambridge monographs.
  Decide whether it stays in the list.
